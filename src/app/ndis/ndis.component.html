WHAT IS NDIS?

The National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS) is run by the National Disability Insurance Agency (NDIA).

Over 4 million people in Australia have a disability. The National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS) offers more than $20 billion dollars in support every year to an estimated 500,000 Australians with a permanent and/or serious disability.

The NDIS defines a permanent impairment as one that will affect a person throughout their lifetime, whereas a substantial disability is one that will have a major impact on a personâ€™s ability to conduct daily activities.

Please visit the NDIS website for further information on eligibility requirements if you or a family member satisfy this condition. The NDIS will evaluate your claim and offer funds for you to receive necessary support based on your customised plan to help you with your daily living.
